    About me

    I am a Ph.D. student at the University of California, Irvine in the Department of Economics. My primary research is in the field of public economics. In particular, I research how social influence affects contributions to public goods. My Master's Thesis examines the effect of contributions to charity made by an individual's reference group on the amount the individual contributes. Currently, I am working with Amihai Glazer, an expert in the field, to revise my thesis for submission to academic journals. My career began in a M.S. in economics program at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte. First, I assisted Dr. Ellen Sewell in her research on fixed book price laws in Europe and the welfare implications of resale price maintenance. Second, I aided Dr. Paul Gaggl in his research on the distributional effect on wages from electricity adoption in the early 20th century. Now, as a member of the Department of Economics at UC Irvine, I am engaged in teaching intermediate economics, and pursuing the advanced study of economics and political economy.
